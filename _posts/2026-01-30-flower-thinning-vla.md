---
title: Behavior Cloning for Robotic Flower Thinning
description: Notes on why agricultural manipulation is a strong testbed for VLA and imitation learning.
---

Agricultural manipulation is a useful domain for robotic learning because the environment is structured enough to instrument, but visually diverse enough to expose the limits of brittle hand-coded rules.

The flower thinning project connects behavior cloning, vision-language-action models, and reinforcement learning. The immediate goal is not only to complete a single motion, but to improve policy behavior across plant variation, occlusion, and long-horizon operation.
