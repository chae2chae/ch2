---
title: From Teleoperation Interfaces to Demonstration Data
description: How simulation and hand-tracking interfaces can support future imitation learning pipelines.
---

Teleoperation is valuable when it can become more than a control interface. A well-designed interface can also produce demonstrations, expose failure cases, and create a bridge from human motion to robot policy learning.

The MuJoCo teleoperation work connected hand tracking, Vive tracking, and Manus glove signals to simulated robot and gripper control. That makes it a useful stepping stone toward collecting better demonstrations for dexterous manipulation.
